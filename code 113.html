Regular Insulin:

Introduction

Regular insulin is a short-acting synthetic human insulin used primarily in the management of diabetes mellitus and hyperglycemia of various etiologies. It mimics endogenous insulin’s physiological effects, mainly lowering serum glucose via stimulating peripheral glucose uptake and inhibiting hepatic glucose production.

This medication is available as:

  • An injectable solution
  • An intravenous (IV) solution
  • An inhalable powder

FDA Approval: Humulin, the first biosynthetic human insulin, was approved on October 28, 1982, after five months of review.

Indications

Labeled Uses: Mainstay therapy for diabetes mellitus type 1 and some cases of type 2 to improve glycemic control.

Off-label Uses Include:

  • Hormonal resuscitation in cadaveric organ recovery
  • Treatment of calcium channel blocker and beta-blocker toxicity
  • Management of severe hyperglycemia and diabetic ketoacidosis (continuous IV infusion dosing guidance included)
  • Refractory gestational diabetes mellitus
  • Management of hyperkalemia (IV insulin with dextrose)

Pharmacology

Mechanism of Action

Stimulates peripheral glucose uptake (skeletal muscle and fat), inhibits hepatic glucose production, lipolysis, and proteolysis, and enhances protein synthesis targeting skeletal muscle, liver, and adipose tissue.

Pharmacokinetics

  • Absorption: Bioavailability 55-77% (subcutaneous), onset 0.5 hr, peak 0.8-2 hr, duration 4–12 hrs (U-100), up to 24 hrs (U-500)
  • Distribution Volume: 0.26-0.36 L/kg
  • Metabolism: Liver (>50%), kidney (~30%), adipose tissue/muscle (~20%)
  • Excretion: Urine

Clinical Use & Administration

Why mostly used in Type 1 Diabetes?

Type 1 Diabetes is caused by autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells, resulting in insulin deficiency. People with T1D rely primarily on exogenous insulin to manage blood glucose levels.

Administration Guidelines:

  1. Preparation: Ensure insulin solution is clear and colorless. Do not use if cloudy or viscous.
  2. Injection: Administer subcutaneously at a 90° angle using a suitable insulin syringe or pen.
  3. Intravenous Use: Use U-100 insulin only. Monitor serum potassium and blood glucose closely during infusion.
  4. Dosing: Follow prescribed dosage exactly; avoid dosing without meals to prevent hypoglycemia.
  5. Storage of opened vials and pens: Follow temperature guidelines to maintain efficacy.
  6. Disposal: Dispose of needles and pens safely to avoid injury or contamination.

Additional Administration Steps (Humulin R):

  1. Choose appropriate syringe or insulin pen compatible with Humulin R.
  2. Inspect the vial or pen for clarity and expiration date; do not use if cloudy or past expiry.
  3. Wash hands and clean injection site with alcohol swab.
  4. If using a vial, roll it gently between palms to mix insulin; do not shake.
  5. Draw up the correct dose by inserting needle into vial and pulling plunger slowly.
  6. Remove air bubbles from syringe by flicking and pushing air out.
  7. Pinch skin to create a fold and inject subcutaneously at 90-degree angle.
  8. Hold needle in place for 5-10 seconds before withdrawal to ensure full dose delivery.
  9. Dispose of syringe or pen needle in proper sharps container.
  10. Rotate injection sites to avoid lipodystrophy.
  11. Monitor blood glucose regularly per medical advice.

Storage Instructions

Humulin R U-100

  • Unopened vials: Refrigerate 2-8°C (36-46°F) until expiration or store room temperature max 31 days; Do not freeze
  • Opened vials: Refrigerate or room temperature ≤30°C (86°F) for up to 31 days; do not freeze

Humulin R U-500

  • Unopened vials: Refrigerate 2-8°C until expiration; can store at room temperature <30°C (86°F) for 40 days; do not shake vial
  • Opened vials: Refrigerate or room temperature ≤30°C, discard after 40 days

Humulin R KwikPen

  • Unopened pens: Refrigerate 2-8°C until expiration; or keep room temperature ≤30°C for max 28 days; protect from heat and light; do not freeze
  • Used pens: Store room temperature ≤30°C, discard after 28 days; do not refrigerate

Novolin R Vials

  • Unopened: Refrigerate 2-8°C until expiration or room temperature ≤25°C (77°F) max 42 days; do not freeze
  • Opened: Store at room temperature ≤25°C; refrigeration not recommended

Pregnancy & Lactation Considerations

Pregnancy Category B: No established association with birth defects or adverse outcomes. Poorly controlled diabetes in pregnancy poses risks to both mother and fetus.

Lactation studies show exogenous insulin transfers into breast milk but no adverse effects reported in infants.

Side Effects & Adverse Reactions

  • Hypersensitivity/allergic reactions (redness, swelling, rash, breathing difficulty)
  • Swelling (edema), weight gain
  • Injection site reactions (pruritus, erythema, skin thickening or hollowing)
  • Hypokalemia (potassium shift causing cramps, arrhythmias)
  • Hypoglycemia (life-threatening if improper dosing)
  • Cardiovascular: peripheral edema

Toxicity & Antidote

Insulin toxicity symptoms relate to hypoglycemia and electrolyte disturbances, possibly causing seizures, coma, hypotension, bradycardia.

Antidote: Dextrose infusion (400-600 mg/kg/hr) and glucagon emergency kit with dextrose for severe toxicity.

Pen Device

Humulin R KwikPen: First FDA-approved prefilled pen device for U-500 strength insulin (500 units/mL) for patients needing >200 units/day.

Additional Pen Device Information:

  1. Humulin R KwikPen allows accurate dosing with easy-to-read increments.
  2. Prefilled and disposable pen designed for patient convenience.
  3. Features a robust and ergonomic design for easy handling.
  4. Requires no mixing or reconstitution prior to use.
  5. Has a maximum dose per injection of 300 units.
  6. Should be stored between 2–8°C before first use and can be kept at room temperature (below 30°C) for 28 days after first use.
  7. Proper disposal of the pen and needles is mandatory to prevent injury.

Inhalation Insulin

Afrezza: Afrezza is an FDA-approved inhaled short-acting insulin powder delivered via a small inhaler device. It is prescribed for adults to improve glycemic control in diabetes mellitus.

Warning: Afrezza should not be used in patients with chronic lung disease such as asthma or COPD due to the risk of acute bronchospasm.

Typical dosing duration is 15 minutes before meals. Patients require pulmonary function testing prior to initiation and periodically during therapy.

Storage: Store unopened cartridges refrigerated; opened packs can be kept at room temperature for up to 10 days.
